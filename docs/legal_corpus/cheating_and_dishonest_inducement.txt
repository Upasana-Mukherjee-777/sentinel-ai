General cheating and dishonest inducement to deliver property (illustrative
summary, cross-check current BNS section numbering before citing in any
real filing): where a person deceives another and thereby dishonestly
induces them to deliver property, money, or valuable security, or to
consent to any person retaining property, this falls under cheating
provisions. UPI fraud, investment scams, loan scams, job scams, and
parcel-scam messages that lead to a victim making a payment are all
patterns of cheating under this general category, often in combination
with the computer-resource-specific identity theft and impersonation
provisions in the IT Act where an electronic medium was used to commit
the deception.
