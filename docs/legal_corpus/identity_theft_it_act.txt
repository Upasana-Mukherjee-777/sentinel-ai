IT Act 2000, provisions on identity theft and impersonation (illustrative
summary, verify exact section number and current text before citing in
any real filing): fraudulent or dishonest use of another person's
electronic signature, password, or other unique identification feature
is treated as identity theft. Cheating by personation using a computer
resource -- for example, impersonating a bank, police officer, or
government official over a call or through a fake message to induce a
victim to hand over money or credentials -- is a distinct, related
offence. Digital arrest scams and fake-KYC/fake-government-notice
message scams both fall under this category, since the core mechanism
is impersonation of an authority through an electronic medium to induce
a fraudulent transfer.
